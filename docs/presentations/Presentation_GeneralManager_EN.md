# WorkAudit — Executive Briefing  
**Audience:** General Manager  
**Format:** Simple slide outline (copy into PowerPoint or similar)

## Slide 1 — Title

**WorkAudit**  
*Bank-grade document management for compliance, audit, and daily operations*

Presenter name · Date

---

## Slide 2 — What it is

**One sentence**  
WorkAudit is a **desktop application** that helps the bank **capture, organize, review, and report on** customer and internal documents in a **controlled, traceable** way.

---

## Slide 3 — Why it matters

**Business outcomes**

- **Compliance:** Supports audit-ready processes — who did what, and when.
- **Consistency:** Same workflows and roles across branches and teams.
- **Visibility:** Reports and dashboards for management (PDF, Excel, scheduled delivery).
- **Risk reduction:** Retention, legal hold, and secure handling aligned with banking expectations.

---

## Slide 4 — What users can do (high level)

| Area | In plain language |
|------|-------------------|
| **Documents** | Import from files, folders, or camera; organize by branch/section/status. |
| **Workflow** | Assignments, approvals, notes — with roles (Admin, Manager, Auditor, Viewer). |
| **Search & reports** | Find documents quickly; generate executive and operational reports. |
| **Audit trail** | Tamper-evident activity history for accountability. |

---

## Slide 5 — Who it is for

- **Branches & operations:** Daily document handling and tasks.  
- **Audit & compliance:** Reviews, evidence, and reporting.  
- **Management:** Summaries, KPIs, and oversight without micromanaging every click.

---

## Slide 6 — Trust & continuity

- **Security:** Strong passwords, encryption, and role-based access — not “everyone sees everything.”  
- **Backup & recovery:** Encrypted backups and documented recovery path for business continuity.  
- **Deployment:** Windows desktop; designed for **per-branch or pilot** rollout and scaling when IT approves.

---

## Slide 7 — Summary

**WorkAudit** turns document-heavy banking work into **structured, auditable, reportable** processes — so leadership gets **clear oversight** and teams get **one consistent way of working**.
